Lifelong infertility increases with higher levels of female education

Professor Jiang Quanbao of the Institute of Population and Development at Xi'an Jiaotong University and researcher at the China Population and Development Research Centre (CPDRC) have released at BMJ Open - "Rising trend of childlessness in China. Analysis of social and regional disparities with The paper, "Rising trend of childlessness in China: Analysis of social and regional disparities with 2010 and 2020 census data", examines the factors associated with the rise in female infertility between 2010 and 2020 and predicts the future trend of the rate, providing data to support policy implementation. The study concludes with a prediction that lifetime infertility among urban and tertiary-educated women will continue to rise, exceeding 10 per cent. As urbanisation accelerates and tertiary education becomes more widely available, the number of women with lifelong infertility will increase more rapidly.
